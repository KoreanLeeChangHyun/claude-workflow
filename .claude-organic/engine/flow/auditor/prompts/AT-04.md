# AT-04: Criteria Measurability

## Evaluation Dimension

Assess whether the `criteria` field contains measurable, binary-checkable
acceptance conditions — i.e., whether each criterion can be automatically or
objectively verified as PASS or FAIL without subjective judgment.

## Scoring Criteria

| Score | Verdict | Criterion |
|-------|---------|-----------|
| 5     | PASS    | ≥ 50% of criteria items contain numeric thresholds, counts, or explicit PASS/FAIL test conditions; criteria are binary-checkable |
| 4     | PASS    | ≥ 40% measurable; remaining items are clearly verifiable (e.g., "file exists") |
| 3     | WARN    | 20–39% measurable; several criteria use vague language ("should work", "looks good") |
| 2     | FAIL    | < 20% measurable; most criteria require subjective judgment |
| 1     | FAIL    | Criteria field is absent, empty, or contains only vague/qualitative statements |

**Note (Core Gate):** This item is a core gate. A FAIL score (≤ 2) forces the
overall Tier-2 verdict to WARN at minimum, even if all other items pass.

## Bias Mitigation

Normalize ratio by total criteria item count (3–9 item range is healthy);
recognize file-existence checks and test-count checks as measurable even without
numeric thresholds.
