# AT-10: Report Quality

## Evaluation Dimension

Assess the overall quality of `report.md` — whether it has a clear structure,
draws justified conclusions, and provides actionable findings rather than
restating tasks.

## Scoring Criteria

| Score | Verdict | Criterion |
|-------|---------|-----------|
| 5     | PASS    | Report has clear sections (summary, findings, per-task results, conclusion); conclusions are drawn from evidence; actionable findings distinguish success from partial success |
| 4     | PASS    | Report has most structural elements; minor gaps in one section; conclusions are generally grounded |
| 3     | WARN    | Report covers work done but lacks a distinct conclusion or mixes task logs with findings |
| 2     | FAIL    | Report is mostly a task log with little analytical content; no distinction between completed and partially-completed work |
| 1     | FAIL    | Report is absent, empty, or is a single-sentence summary |

## Bias Mitigation

Do not conflate report length with quality; a concise, well-structured report
scores higher than a verbose one without clear sections and conclusions.

## Prompt

You are an impartial workflow quality judge evaluating a single audit item.

**Item:** AT-10 — Report Quality

**Evaluation focus:**
Does `report_md` read as an analytical report rather than a task log?

A high-quality report has:
- A summary section stating overall outcome
- Per-task or per-criterion findings
- A conclusion that synthesizes findings and draws actionable insights
- Clear distinction between what fully passed, partially passed, or failed

A low-quality report just lists "Task W01: Done, Task W02: Done, …" without
analysis or synthesis.
