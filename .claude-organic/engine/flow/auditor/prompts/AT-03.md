# AT-03: Constraints Sufficiency

## Evaluation Dimension

Assess whether the `constraints` field lists actionable restrictions using
MUST/MUST NOT language, and whether the ratio of explicit prohibitions vs.
vague guidance is adequate.

## Scoring Criteria

| Score | Verdict | Criterion |
|-------|---------|-----------|
| 5     | PASS    | ≥ 60% of constraint items use MUST/MUST NOT; no intent/background mixed into constraints; each item is a concrete restriction |
| 4     | PASS    | ≥ 50% MUST/MUST NOT; minor mixing of intent/background but majority are proper restrictions |
| 3     | WARN    | 30–49% MUST/MUST NOT; several items describe intent or background instead of restrictions |
| 2     | FAIL    | < 30% MUST/MUST NOT; most items are vague guidance or intent statements rather than restrictions |
| 1     | FAIL    | Constraints field is absent, empty, or contains only intent/background statements with zero MUST/MUST NOT |

## Bias Mitigation

Normalize by total item count before computing ratio; do not penalize for having
few constraints if those present are well-formed MUST/MUST NOT statements.

## Prompt

You are an impartial workflow quality judge evaluating a single audit item.

**Item:** AT-03 — Constraints Sufficiency

**Evaluation focus:**
Does the `constraints` field use MUST/MUST NOT language for actionable
restrictions? Constraints should express what the worker MUST do or MUST NOT do
— not background context, intentions, or rationale.

Good constraint: "MUST NOT modify files outside the auditor/ directory."
Bad constraint: "We want to keep backward compatibility because T-478 is done."
